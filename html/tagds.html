The Adecco Group Design System

The Adecco Group's multi-brand design system encompasses all digital platforms within the group.

Context and goals

At the core of The Adecco Group's digital transformation was the recognition of the need for a unified Design System, named TAG DS. This system aimed to standardize UI/UX components across all digital platforms, enhancing visual cohesion, usability, and accessibility. It facilitated the development of digital products for the group's various brands, such as Adecco, Akkodis, and LHH, streamlining processes and ensuring a consistent user experience across the brand portfolio.

Challenges

One major challenge was the diversity of existing user interfaces, developed in isolation by different teams, leading to a lack of coherence and variable accessibility standards. Conducting a comprehensive audit of the existing platforms was particularly challenging due to their disparate construction. This variability posed a significant obstacle in understanding the current state and planning for the cohesive integration of the Design System.

My Role

As a Product Designer and Design System Contributor, my role was to coordinate the collaborative effort among designers, developers, and stakeholders to lay the groundwork for the design system, from the Discovery phase through to the final Construct phase. This involved documentation and publication on Zeroheight, ensuring the system met both design and business objectives.

Design Process

Research and Analisys

The process began with an exhaustive research and analysis phase, including interviews with stakeholders, analysis of competitors' design systems, and workshops with User Personas defined by the Research team. This was aimed at understanding their needs and pain points.

Definition of Design Principles

Principles of design centered on accessibility, reusability, and simplicity were established. These principles were crucial in the creation of all components, including the Time Picker, ensuring they were intuitive and accessible to all users.

Tools and software

Figma was used for design and prototyping collaboration, Zeroheight for component documentation, and Storybook for documenting and sharing system components with developers.

Time picker Component creation

Justificaci贸n del Componente

The Time Picker was designed to address the need for efficient and accessible time input in time management and scheduling applications. The lack of consistency in previous time input solutions had resulted in a fragmented user experience.

Design Process and development

The design of the Time Picker started with initial sketches and wireframes, followed by interactive prototypes in Figma. Usability testing was conducted to gather feedback, leading to several design iterations to improve ease of use and accessibility. Close collaboration with developers ensured effective technical implementation consistent with the design.

Integration and Standards

The component was rigorously tested to ensure its compatibility with WCAG accessibility standards and its smooth integration with other components of the design system, maintaining visual and functional cohesion.

Results and Impact

Adoption and Feedback

Since its launch, the TAG DS has been widely adopted by product teams, with the Time Picker receiving praise for its ease of use and accessibility. The design system has facilitated UI consistency across platforms and accelerated the development of new products. The Usability Measurement Score has increased from 45% with the first version of the TAG DS V.0 design system to 72% with the latest version, TAG DS V.1.

Usability Measurement Score de TAG DS

Lessons Learned

The process underscored the importance of interdisciplinary collaboration and user feedback in the development of design systems. It was also learned that clear and accessible documentation is key to the adoption and maintenance of the system.

The Future of the Design System

Based on continuous feedback, the design system is constantly evolving, with plans to expand its component library and improve design guidelines to adapt to new technologies and user needs.

Conclusion

The creation of The Adecco Group's Design System has been a journey of collaboration, innovation, and learning. Through the creation of components such as the Time Picker, we have established a new standard of design and development within the company, demonstrating the power of a well-executed design system to transform the user experience and facilitate product development.
