SMOWLTECH

Smowltech Platform

Improving live monitoring, accessibility, and incident review workflows for a proctoring SaaS

Introduction

Context and Objectives

Smowltech provides online proctoring solutions used by universities and training platforms. The product includes live monitoring, technical status signals (camera, audio, lock tools), and a review flow for incidents generated during exams. My role focused on improving usability and accessibility across key workflows, while scaling UI consistency through a shared design system.

Challenges

Operators needed to make fast decisions under pressure, but the UI contained dense information, inconsistent patterns, and accessibility gaps. This increased cognitive load during live exams and slowed down incident review afterwards.

The goal was to make critical monitoring signals easier to scan, reduce errors, and ensure core flows were accessible (keyboard, focus visibility, contrast, and assistive technology friendly) without slowing the product down.

Design Process

Research and Analysis

I worked with product and engineering to map the most critical moments during an exam (live monitoring) and the highest-friction steps after an exam (incident review). We audited current UI patterns, gathered operator pain points, and validated requirements against accessibility best practices (keyboard navigation, focus management, contrast, and clear status messaging).

Solution

Improvement of User Experience

I introduced clearer information hierarchy for live monitoring (what matters now vs. what can wait), standardized components through the design system, and strengthened accessibility basics (visible focus, consistent labels, predictable keyboard order, and readable status messaging). For incident review, the goal was to make evidence easier to interpret and quicker to act on.

Results and Impact

The improvements reduced cognitive load for operators, made core flows more consistent, and increased accessibility coverage across key screens. Internally, shared components and patterns improved design-to-dev handoff and helped teams iterate faster without re-solving the same UI problems.

Lessons Learned

Operational products need ruthless prioritization of “what matters right now.” Accessibility and usability become dramatically easier to maintain when patterns are standardized and validated early (instead of fixed later). Tight collaboration with engineering was essential to keep changes safe and incremental.

Conclusion

This case study highlights how improving hierarchy, consistency, and accessibility can make high-pressure workflows safer and faster. By strengthening the foundations (design system + accessibility practices) and targeting the most critical moments (live monitoring + incident review), Smowltech can scale features while keeping the experience usable for everyone.
